>
> 2\. **Binding a phone number to WABA**: [Click here to view the number binding tutorial.](/help/whatsapp-accounts-management/create-a-whatsapp-api-account/add-numbers)

## Create a New Account

After completing the YCloud account registration, you can create a WhatsApp account on the homepage.

For the text tutorial, please refer to the video tutorial: [https://www.bilibili.com/video/BV1Aa4y1g7di/](https://www.bilibili.com/video/BV1Aa4y1g7di/)

### Step 1: Click the Create Button

Visit the [WhatsApp accounts](https://www.ycloud.com/console/#/app/dashboard/account) page and click "Get started" on WhatsApp Business API

![](/images/gitbook/image-77-5c1ae6f1.png)

View the guide and scroll to the button of the page and click "I'm ready to start."

![](/images/gitbook/image-78-929055cf.png)

### Step 2: Log in to Facebook

After clicking login, a Facebook authorization popup will appear. Log in with the Facebook account you prepared. If the account is incorrect, you can click "Log in another account" to switch. Confirm the account is correct and click "Continue" for the next step.

![](/images/gitbook/image-172-1-4ebc97ed.png)

*Login Facebook*

### Step 3: Confirm the Agreement

Click "Get started" to continue creating.

![](/images/gitbook/image-173-1-a251a9c7.png)

*Policy*

### Step 4: Create a BM Account

Enter your company's information:

1. Business name: The company name which exactly same with business license. Please do not enter company abbreviation or brand name.
2. Business email address: It is recommended to use an email address that matches your website domain.
3. Business website or profile page: The company's URL. Note that the company's URL must be https.
4. Country: The country where the company operates. Make sure to select the country where your company is registered for certification. For example, if you are submitting an Indonesian business license for corporate certification, select Indonesia.

> **Info**
>
> If you already have a BM account, you can select it in the Business portfolio option.

![](/images/gitbook/image-174-1-d71c229a.png)

*Create BM*

### Step 5:  Start with a new WhatsApp phone number

![](/images/gitbook/image-79-eb8b9851.png)


### Step 6: Create a WABA

Select/create a new WABA. If you already have a WABA, you can select it from the dropdown, otherwise, choose to create one.

![](/images/gitbook/image-162-1-c60b67cb.png)

*Create WABA*

### Step 7: Set WABA and Number Information

1. WhatsApp business account name: The name of the WABA. This is for internal business differentiation and will not be visible to your audience on your WhatsApp account profile.
2. WhatsApp business display name: The name of the number. The display name will show to your customers and should be related to your company or brand name. Click to view [Display Name Guidelines](https://www.facebook.com/business/help/757569725593362).
3. Category: Industry

![](/images/gitbook/image-80-1ea2ac6f.png)

### Step 8: Add phone number

Enter the phone number you have prepared.

![](/images/gitbook/image-81-494a4ef4.png)

Enter the code you have received

![](/images/gitbook/image-82-c1657f12.png)

### Step 9: Creation Successful

A prompt indicating successful creation will appear. Be sure to click the "Finish" button at the bottom of the popup.

![](/images/gitbook/image-168-1-b88e333d.png)

*WABA created*

## Frequently Asked Questions

<details>

<summary>Can numbers from Mainland China (+86) be registered?</summary>

Yes, as long as the phone numbers can receive verification codes successfully, they can be registered.

</details>

<details>

<summary>Network errors such as "Network error" occur during the creation process</summary>

It is recommended to go back one step, or close the popup and restart the process. Check if there are already created WABAs in the BM and WABA dropdowns.

</details>

<details>

<summary>The number binding prompts that this number has already been registered with a WhatsApp account</summary>

Carefully read the prompt, which may indicate two situations:

1. This number has already been registered with WhatsApp or WhatsApp Business . In this case, you need to delete the original account and wait for some time before you can use it to register for a WhatsApp API account. Since deleting the account will result in the loss of historical chat records, we recommend you change to a new number for registration.
2. This number has already been registered with a WhatsApp API account and is under your current BM account. In this case, the prompt will guide you through the migration process, requiring you to turn off the two-step verification for this number. Please follow the instructions to complete the migration. For detailed procedures, please refer to the WhatsApp API migration documentation.

</details>

<details>

<summary>What if I can't receive the verification code?</summary>

In addition to SMS, you can try using voice to receive it. For Mainland China (+86) numbers, it is suitable to use voice to receive it. If multiple requests fail, it is recommended to change the number for registration.

</details>
